Darin Tsui

Hi there! My name is Darin. I'm a senior undergraduate student at the University of California San Diego studying Biomedical Engineering. My research focuses on developing engineering-focused solutions for applications of healthcare.

I am currently researching the design and development of a surgical positioning platform for minimally invasive surgery under Dr. Frank Talke in the Talke Biomedical Device Lab. Concurrently, I also am exploring applications of machine learning in bioelectronic COVID-19 detection under Dr. Gert Cauwenberghs in the Integrated Systems Neuroengineering Laboratory.

Outside of research, I am also the President of the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE) student branch at UC San Diego. In addition to managing club logistics, I also volunteer my time as a project mentor throughout the academic year. Some of the projects I've mentored include creating a flow sensor, building an Android app for social networking, and conducting machine learning analysis on Spotify recommended songs.

Projects

  • Brain Tumor Classification

    This project attempts to classify brain tumors from MRI images given various machine learning classifiers using models of various complexities. The models explored in this project include: convolutional neural networks (CNN), principal component analysis (PCA) with CNN, and K-Nearest Neighbors (KNN).

  • Motor Imagery Classification

    Motor imagery is the action of thinking about a movement without actually moving your body. This project aims to classify motor imagery movements using classical machine learning algorithms.
